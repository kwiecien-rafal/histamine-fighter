## Two enzymes

The body breaks histamine down with two main enzymes. Diamine oxidase (DAO)
works outside cells, mostly in the lining of the small intestine, and is the main
route for clearing histamine that comes from food. Histamine N-methyltransferase
(HNMT) works inside cells and handles histamine produced within the body. In
histamine intolerance, the food-facing DAO route is the one usually implicated.

## What lowers DAO activity

DAO capacity is not fixed. It can be reduced by damage or inflammation in the gut,
by some medications, and by other biogenic amines competing for the same enzyme.
Alcohol is notable because it both adds to the histamine load and can interfere
with breakdown, which is why drinks like wine and beer are often poorly tolerated.

When DAO activity is low, histamine that would normally be cleared in the gut
instead reaches the bloodstream, where it can act on receptors throughout the body
and produce symptoms.

## Other biogenic amines

Histamine is one of several biogenic amines formed in food. Others, such as
tyramine, putrescine, and cadaverine, are produced by the same kinds of bacterial
activity during ageing and fermentation. They matter because they compete with
histamine for DAO, so a food high in other amines can worsen tolerance even if its
own histamine content is modest.

## Important note

This article is educational and is not medical advice. Tests of DAO activity
exist but their interpretation is debated; discuss them with a clinician rather
than self-diagnosing.
